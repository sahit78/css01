ANIME

Anime is a Japanese style of animation that is featured in many popular TV programs and films.
The contemporary anime style originated in the 1960s in Japan when artists who drew manga-distinctly Japanese comics-began to translate their work into animation for TV and film.
This new style became immensely popular in Japan, and by the 1980s, the popularity of anime had begun to spread to other countries. By the beginning of this century, anime was one of the most popular styles of animation worldwide.

History of Anime

The history of anime dates back to the early 20th century, with the earliest verifiable films emerging in 1917.
Before film, Japan had rich traditions of storytelling through emakimono (scrolls with illustrated stories) and kagee (shadow plays).

Pros of ANIME

Exploration of Deeper Themes: Anime allows creators to delve into complex topics, multi-layered characters, and philosophical themes beyond typical animation.
Vivid Imagery: From epic battles to breathtaking landscapes, anime offers visually stunning and imaginative scenes.
Nuanced Characters: Anime characters often have depth, growth, and relatable emotions.
Diverse Genres: Anime spans genres like romance, sci-fi, fantasy, and horror, catering to a wide audience.
Global Impact: It has become a global phenomenon, transcending cultural boundaries

Cons of ANIME

Stigma and Stereotypes: Some people associate anime with violence, or childish content.
Inconsistent Quality: Not all anime shows maintain high standards; some suffer from poor animation or weak storytelling.
Time Commitment: Long-running series can be addictive but time-consuming.
Cultural Differences: Cultural references and context may not always translate well for international viewers.

Fan response

Anime clubs gave rise to anime conventions in the 1990s with the "anime boom", a period marked by anime's increased global popularity.
These conventions are dedicated to anime and manga and include elements like cosplay contests and industry talk panels.
According to Crunchyroll's research data from 2023 to 2024 provided by its President Rahul Parini, revealed that there are approximately 800 million people globally (outside of China and Japan) whom are either highly aware of anime, show interest in anime or currently watch anime and identify as fans.
